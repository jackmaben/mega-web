01Workflow Audit

Workflow Audit & AI Readiness Assessment

We begin every engagement with a structured discovery phase before any code is written. Our team audits your current workflows, existing software (CRM, ERP, SaaS tools, legacy systems), data sources, and API infrastructure to identify where AI can deliver measurable value.

This phase produces a written AI Readiness Report covering:

  • Automation gaps and workflow bottlenecks ranked by impact
  • Data quality assessment and any preparation required before integration
  • API availability, access constraints, and legacy system compatibility
  • GDPR compliance considerations under Articles 5, 25, and 32, including data minimisation, privacy by design, and security of processing
Why this matters: 67% of AI projects that fail do so because of poor data readiness or unclear use-case scope (McKinsey, 2024). Our discovery phase eliminates both risks before development begins.
02Architecture Design

AI Architecture Design & Integration Planning

Once the audit is complete, our engineers design a scalable AI architecture tailored to your existing software environment. We do not build generic solutions; every architecture is mapped to your specific tools and workflows.

This phase covers:

  • API orchestration strategy (REST, GraphQL, webhooks, middleware layers)
  • Model selection and LLM routing including OpenAI GPT-4o, Azure OpenAI, and open-source models where data privacy requires on-premise deployment
  • RAG system design using vector databases such as Pinecone or Weaviate for internal knowledge retrieval
  • Middleware and integration layer planning for CRM platforms (HubSpot, Salesforce), ERP systems (SAP, Microsoft Dynamics), and custom SaaS tools
  • Modular architecture to support phased rollout and future AI expansion

You receive a documented integration architecture and phased delivery roadmap before development starts so scope, timeline, and budget are agreed before a single line of code is written.

03Development

AI Integration Development & Build

Our engineering team builds the AI-powered automation using sprint-based delivery, with working software reviewed at the end of every two-week sprint. You see progress continuously not at the end of a long development phase.

Typical build activities include:

  • ETL pipeline development for legacy systems with structured or unstructured data
  • Embedding pipelines and document indexing for RAG-based knowledge systems
  • AI agent development with tool-calling, approval logic, and audit trail integration
  • Webhook and event-driven triggers for cross-platform workflow automation

All development follows GDPR-aligned data handling practices, with access controls, data minimisation, and processing records built in from the start not added as an afterthought.

04Testing & Validation

Testing, Validation & Sandbox Deployment

Before any AI workflow touches your live systems, we validate it in a controlled test environment that mirrors your production setup. This step protects operational continuity and catches integration issues before they affect your team or customers.

Our testing process covers:

  • Functional testing of every AI workflow trigger, output, and edge case
  • Hallucination testing and response accuracy validation for RAG and LLM outputs
  • Load and performance testing to confirm the integration handles real-world data volumes
  • Security and access-control testing against your permission rules and GDPR requirements
  • User acceptance testing (UAT) with a pilot group from your team before full rollout

We do not move to deployment until UAT is passed and you have signed off on results. No surprises.

09 FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Answers to the most common questions Irish businesses ask before starting an AI integration project — from CRM compatibility to cost, timelines, data readiness, and post-launch support.

Can AI integration work with our existing CRM?

Yes — AI integration works directly with your existing CRM without replacing it. We connect AI capabilities to your CRM through its native API, using permission-based data access so AI tools only read or write what your rules allow. This means AI assistants, copilots, reporting automation, and workflow triggers operate inside your current CRM environment — not alongside a separate system you need to manage.

We have integrated AI with HubSpot, Salesforce, Microsoft Dynamics 365, Pipedrive, and Zoho CRM. If your CRM has an API (most modern platforms do), AI integration is feasible. For legacy CRMs with limited API access, we build middleware wrappers that create the connectivity without modifying your core system.

Most CRM AI integrations are live within 4–8 weeks from discovery sign-off.

How much do AI integration services cost in Ireland?

AI integration projects in Ireland typically range from €8,000 to €75,000+ depending on scope, system complexity, and the number of workflows being automated.

As a general guide:

  • €8,000 – €20,000 — Single-workflow AI automation, AI chatbot integration, or a standalone copilot connected to one data source. Typically 4–8 weeks delivery.
  • €20,000 – €45,000 — Multi-system AI integration (CRM + ERP, or CRM + internal knowledge base), RAG system build, or AI agent with approval workflows. Typically 8–16 weeks delivery.
  • €45,000 – €75,000+ — Enterprise AI integration across multiple platforms, legacy system modernisation with AI layer, or full AI workflow automation programme across departments. Typically 16–24 weeks delivery.

These ranges cover discovery, architecture, development, testing, and deployment. Post-launch support and optimisation is quoted separately on a retainer or time-and-materials basis.

A discovery workshop (typically 2–3 hours) produces a detailed scope, timeline, and fixed-price proposal before any development begins — so you have a committed budget, not an estimate.

How long does AI integration usually take?

Most AI integration projects take 6–20 weeks from discovery to production deployment, depending on complexity.

Realistic timelines by project type:

  • 4–8 weeks — Single AI workflow automation, chatbot integration with one knowledge source, or AI reporting layer on an existing dashboard
  • 8–14 weeks — multi-system integration (e.g. AI copilot connected to CRM + internal documents), RAG system with document indexing, or AI agents with approval logic
  • 14–20 weeks — Enterprise-wide AI integration, legacy software modernisation, or phased rollout across multiple departments or user groups

The biggest factors that extend timelines are data readiness issues discovered during audit, API access restrictions on existing software, and internal approval processes for security or compliance sign-off. Our discovery phase identifies all of these before development starts, so timelines we commit to in the proposal reflect your actual environment — not a best-case scenario.

Do we need clean data before starting AI integration?

No — you do not need perfectly clean data before starting. Most Irish businesses we work with have messy, inconsistent, or partially structured data, and AI integration can still proceed.

What matters is understanding your data's current state before development begins, not fixing everything upfront. Our discovery phase includes a data quality assessment that identifies:

  • Which data sources are reliable enough to use immediately
  • Which fields need mapping, standardisation, or light cleaning before AI can use them accurately
  • Where data gaps exist that would reduce AI output quality — and whether those gaps matter for your specific use case

For RAG systems and AI knowledge bases, document quality matters more than database cleanliness. For workflow automation and CRM integrations, field consistency and API access matter most.

In practice, most projects begin with the cleanest 20% of your data — proving the AI integration works — then expand as data quality improves through normal business operations.

Can AI features be added in phases?

Yes — phased AI delivery is our recommended approach for most Irish businesses, particularly those integrating AI into live business systems for the first time.

A typical phased delivery looks like this:

  • Phase 1 — Pilot (weeks 1–8): One high-value use case, limited to a small user group. Proves the integration works, validates AI outputs with real users, and builds internal confidence before broader rollout.
  • Phase 2 — Expansion (weeks 9–16): Additional workflows, more users, or connection to a second system. Built on the validated architecture from Phase 1 — no rework required.
  • Phase 3 — Scale (weeks 17+): Full deployment, advanced AI features (agents, multi-step automation, predictive reporting), and ongoing optimisation based on usage data.

This approach manages budget in stages, reduces the risk of disrupting live operations, and gives your team time to adapt to AI-assisted workflows before the next phase begins. It also means Phase 1 ROI is visible before you commit to Phase 2 spend.

Can AI be used for internal staff workflows?

Yes — internal AI workflow automation is one of the highest-ROI use cases for Irish businesses, particularly for teams spending significant time on manual admin, data entry, approval routing, or information retrieval.

Common internal AI workflow applications we build:

  • AI copilots embedded in your existing tools (CRM, ERP, project management platforms) that surface relevant information, draft responses, and suggest next actions based on workflow context
  • Internal knowledge-base search powered by RAG — staff ask questions in plain language and receive accurate answers drawn from approved company documents, policies, and SOPs
  • Approval automation — AI reviews submissions against defined criteria, routes to the correct approver, and flags exceptions without manual triage
  • Reporting automation — AI aggregates data from multiple systems and generates structured weekly or monthly reports, reducing manual spreadsheet work
  • Document processing — AI extracts key fields from invoices, contracts, or forms and writes them to the correct system record automatically

Businesses that automate internal admin workflows with AI report a reduction in manual processing time of 30–70% for targeted tasks, depending on current process maturity and data quality.

Do you support OpenAI and Azure OpenAI integration?

Yes — we support both OpenAI and Azure OpenAI integration, and help you choose between them based on your specific requirements.

OpenAI (api.openai.com) suits businesses where data residency is flexible, cost-per-token efficiency is the priority, and access to the latest model releases (GPT-4o, o1, o3) matters for output quality.

Azure OpenAI suits businesses with stricter data residency requirements, existing Microsoft infrastructure (Azure, M365, Dynamics), enterprise SLA requirements, or regulatory environments where EU-hosted processing is required for GDPR compliance.

We also support integration with open-source and self-hosted LLMs — including Mistral, LLaMA, and Gemma — for use cases where data must not leave your own infrastructure. This is most common in healthcare, legal, and financial services contexts where data sensitivity is highest.

In most projects, we recommend Azure OpenAI for Irish enterprise clients due to its EU data residency options (Ireland and Netherlands regions), Microsoft enterprise agreement compatibility, and stronger audit logging for regulated industries.

Can you support the AI system after launch?

Yes — we provide structured post-launch support covering performance monitoring, output tuning, cost management, and ongoing optimisation.

Post-launch support is available on two tracks:

  • Retainer support (recommended): Monthly fixed-fee engagement covering performance monitoring, output quality reviews, prompt and model tuning, usage analysis, and a monthly video call reviewing results and priorities. Most clients stay on retainer for 3–12 months post-launch while AI workflows mature.
  • Time-and-materials support: Ad-hoc support for specific issues, new feature additions, or periodic audits. Suits businesses with internal technical capacity who need occasional specialist input.

What we monitor and improve post-launch:

  • AI output accuracy and hallucination rates — tracked against a baseline set at go-live
  • Workflow completion rates — percentage of automated tasks completing without human intervention
  • LLM token usage and infrastructure costs — optimised monthly to prevent cost drift as usage scales
  • User adoption rates — identifying where staff are bypassing AI workflows and why
  • Model and prompt performance — updated as new model versions release or business requirements change

Clients on our post-launch retainer programme see an average 35% improvement in AI workflow efficiency within 90 days of go-live, as outputs are tuned against real operational data.
